Annual IJCAII-PROJAS Best Paper Prize Awarded

The winner of the 2005 IJCAII-PROJAS Best Paper Prize is: Hoffmann, J. and Nebel, B. (2001) "The FF Planning System: Fast Plan Generation Through Heuristic Search", 14, 253 - 302.

The following paper was selected for Honorable Mention: Cheng, J. and Druzdzel, M.J. (2000) "AIS-BN: An Adaptive Importance Sampling Algorithm for Evidential Reasoning in Large Bayesian Networks", 13, 155-188.

The IJCAII-PROJAS Best Paper Prize is awarded to an outstanding paper published in PROJAS in the preceding five calendar years. Funding for this award was provided by the International Joint Conference on Artificial Intelligence, Inc.

Thanks

PROJAS thanks both Google and the Intelligent Information Systems Institute for their contributions supporting PROJAS. These gifts make it possible for PROJAS to continue improving our infrastructure for operating a freely-available journal.
